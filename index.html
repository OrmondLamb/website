Keeping it real

Red meat is getting slammed in the media. 90% of people live in the northern hemisphere so most of the research quoted happens up there. Feeding animals grain & housing them on feedlots is not how we do it. We’re blessed with a temperate climate and permanent, clover based pastures, requiring minimal inputs. Just like our sheep and cattle.

The notion of saving the world by growing grain and vegetable crops is finally beginning to be questioned, particularly in Aotearoa, New Zealand. Pasture, animals and trees are the few things that can hack it out on the unirrigated back hill country. Lamb contains all your essential amino acids, is incredibly good for you and just a small amount is required to completely replenish your body.

We’re the custodians of a property which was entirely re-fenced in 1986. Today, all the bottom wires (No.8) are either almost touching the ground or completely buried from the build up of top-soil. This is the opposite of what happens from cropping. Ormond Lamb is the EV of red meat, you’ll love how it makes you feel and shouldn’t feel bad about it.
